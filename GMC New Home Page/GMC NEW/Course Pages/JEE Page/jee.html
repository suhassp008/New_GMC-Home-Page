JEE (Joint Entrance Examination)
Joint Entrance Examination has two levels namely JEE MAINS and JEE ADV. This examination is conducted for the students who aspire to get into prestigious or reputed universities like IIT’s or IIST. JEE Mains is conducted for the students who aspire to have their career in Engineering (B. Tech/ B.E). This entrance examination is conducted by the National Test Agency which is an Indian Government Agency approved by the Union of Council of Ministers established in 2017.
This examination has III papers. It is conducted twice in a year. The decision of the examination to be conducted can vary based on certain circumstances such as Pandemic.
Paper 1: Engineering and Technology courses.
Paper 2: Architecture courses. The examination is conducted via computer-based test mode. Drawing test for architecture students is conducted in an offline mode or pen and paper mode.
Paper 3: Planning courses separately.
The examination is conducted for 3 subjects Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics. The language of the paper is in Assamese, Bengali, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu, Urdu in addition to Hindi, English and Gujarati.

Paper 1:
It consists of 75 questions. The maximum marks are 360.
The examination is divided into 3 sections viz. Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics, having 25 questions each.
Paper 2:
The examination consists of 3 parts viz. Part 1, Part 2 and Part 3.
Part 1: It has 30 questions of Mathematics, each awarding 4 marks.
Part 2: It is an Aptitude test, having 50 questions, each awarding 4 marks.
Part 3: It consists of 2 questions carrying 70 marks each to be attempted on separate drawing sheet.
Paper 3:
JEE Main paper 3 is divided into three sections, Part- 1 as Mathematics, Part-2 as General Aptitude and Part-3 as Planning. Part 1 and Part 2 are common for students of both B.Arch. and B. Plan. Part 3 contains questions from drawing for B.Arch. students while for B. Plan students, Part-3 will have general questions from planning. Student's appearing for both B.Arch. and B. Planning i.e., Paper 2 and Paper 3 simultaneously, they will be allotted additional 30 mins after 3 hrs. for B.Arch. examination.

There is a criterion for getting qualified for JEE advanced. A student must clear the JEE Mains where there will be a certain percentile cut-off.
The student can apply for NIT if he/she clears JEE Mains depending on the percentile cutoff. In case the cutoff to write JEE advanced is not met, the student can still apply for NIT’s. also, if a student is qualified to write JEE advanced and not selected for IIT a student can still apply for NIT.
